  <Tab title="Anchor texts">
    <CardGroup cols={1}>
      <Card title="Competing in the Age of AI" subtitle="Marco Iansiti and Karim Lakhani, 2020 -- Harvard Business School Press">
        The clearest strategic treatment of what AI actually changes about how businesses operate. Not technical. [Buy on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/dp/1633697622?tag=noprereqs-20)
      </Card>
      <Card title="Prediction Machines" subtitle="Ajay Agrawal, Joshua Gans and Avi Goldfarb, 2018">
        Reframes AI as a drop in the cost of prediction. Extremely useful for executive decision-making. [Buy on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/dp/1647824672?tag=noprereqs-20)
      </Card>
      <Card title="Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI" subtitle="Ethan Mollick, 2024 -- Portfolio/Penguin">
        The most widely read current entry point on generative AI for knowledge workers and executives. Read it for the practitioner mindset. His free Substack, One Useful Thing, is more current and worth following alongside it. [Buy on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/dp/0593716717?tag=noprereqs-20)
      </Card>
      <Card title="More Human" subtitle="Rasmus Hougaard and Jacqueline Carter -- Harvard Business Review Press, 2025">
        Based on in-depth interviews with more than 100 CEOs and executives. Argues that the leaders who will thrive are those who use AI to become more human, not less. [Buy on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=more+human+hougaard+carter+2025)
      </Card>
      <Card title="Governing the Machine" subtitle="Ray Eitel-Porter, Paul Dongha and Miriam Vogel -- Bloomsbury Business, 2025">
        A practical roadmap for leaders deploying AI while managing legal, operational, and reputational risks. Addresses nine core risk areas including fairness, bias, privacy, and accountability. Foreword by Andrew Ng. [Buy on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=governing+the+machine+eitel-porter)
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
