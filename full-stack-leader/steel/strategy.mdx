<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Anchor texts">
    <CardGroup cols={1}>
      <Card title="Good Strategy, Bad Strategy" subtitle="Richard Rumelt, 2011">
        The clearest writing on what strategy actually is. His kernel framework -- diagnosis, guiding policy, coherent actions -- cuts through most of what gets called strategy in organizations. [Buy on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/dp/0307886239?tag=noprereqs-20)
      </Card>
      <Card title="Playing to Win" subtitle="Roger Martin and A.G. Lafley, 2013">
        A concise framework for making real strategic choices: where to play, how to win. Written by a former P&G CEO. Directly applicable. [Buy on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/dp/1422187683?tag=noprereqs-20)
      </Card>
      <Card title='"What Is Strategy?"' subtitle="Michael Porter -- Harvard Business Review, 1996">
        Still the most rigorous single piece on the subject. Read after Rumelt -- Porter is more analytical, Rumelt more practical. [Free via Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=what+is+strategy+porter+1996)
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Supplement">
    <CardGroup cols={1}>
      <Card title='"Strategy as Simple Rules"' subtitle="Eisenhardt and Sull -- HBR, 2001">
        Useful counterweight to Porter's complexity. [Free via Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=strategy+as+simple+rules+eisenhardt+sull+2001)
      </Card>
      <Card title='"Coevolving"' subtitle="Eisenhardt and Galunic -- HBR, 2000">
        Practically useful for complex organizations managing multiple business units. [Free via Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=coevolving+eisenhardt+galunic+2000)
      </Card>
      <Card title="Cambridge JBS Insights -- Strategy" subtitle="Free at jbs.cam.ac.uk">

      </Card>
